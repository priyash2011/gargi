Introduction

India has a rich history filled with brave women, great scholars, and valuable historical artifacts. This project highlights their contributions to our nation.

Indian Brave Women

Rani Lakshmibai

Rani Lakshmibai of Jhansi was a brave queen who fought against the British in the Revolt of 1857. She is remembered for her courage and patriotism.

Rani Durgawati

Rani Durgawati was a Gond queen who fought bravely to protect her kingdom from the Mughal army.

Velu Nachiyar

Velu Nachiyar was the first Indian queen to fight against British rule.

Begum Hazrat Mahal

She played an important role in the 1857 freedom struggle.

Noor Jahan

Noor Jahan was a powerful Mughal empress known for her intelligence and administrative skills.

Abbakka Chowta

Abbakka Chowta resisted Portuguese rule on the western coast of India.

Indian Scholars

Gargi Vachaknavi

Gargi was a famous philosopher who took part in debates during the Vedic age.

Maitreyi

Maitreyi was a Vedic scholar who believed in knowledge over wealth.

Ghosha

Ghosha was a woman sage who composed hymns in the Rigveda.

Leelavati

Leelavati is a famous Indian mathematical text.

Asima Chatterjee

Asima Chatterjee was a renowned Indian scientist in organic chemistry.
