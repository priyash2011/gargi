Indian women warriors

Rani Lakshmi Bai (born Manikarnika Tambe) was a legendary Indian queen of the Maratha princely state of Jhansi,and an icon of Indian nationalism and courage, famously fighting the British East India Company to protect her kingdom, symbolized by her defiance and death in battle in 1858

Rani Durgavati was the queen regent of Gondwana in 1550-1564 AD. She married Dalpat Shah, the son of King Sangram Shah of Gondwana. She served as regent of Gondwana during the minority of her son, Vir Narayan, from 1550 until 1564. She is chiefly remembered for defending Gondwana against the Mughal Empire.


She was princess of Ramanathapuram (Tamil Nadu) and child of ruler of Ramnad kingdom.
